  ilk: Flipping

Flipping

For flipping, the origin should probably right at the middle of the image. The single argument to "-t flip" is the direction of the reflection vector; things move along this vector. Thus, "-t flip:0" means flip along the X axis, and "-t flip:90" means flip along the Y axis. 
ilk -i init.ppm -0 u:0.5,0.5 -t flip:0 | tojpg > flipx.jpg

ilk -i init.ppm -0 u:0.5,0.5 -t flip:90 | tojpg > flipy.jpg
